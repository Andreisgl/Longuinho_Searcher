I argue that until the 1960s secularisation was widely accepted as an intellectual paradigm for the self-understanding of Western societies, but a theory of secularisation didn't exist at the time. Interestingly, theories of secularisation began to flourish from the 1960s - precisely at the moment when societies lost their certainty with regard to their secular future. They are latecomers, and it is thus not surprising that, once they have been developed, they immediately provoke a backlash at the theoretical and the ideological level. In: Zeitschrift für Soziologie 45, 2016, 219-240.2016 • Detlef PollackModernization Theory - Revised: Designing a Theory of Modern Societies Abstract Attempts to overcome the modernization theory by surrendering its fundamental differentia-tions have turned out to be little successful. In many cases criticism on the modernization the-ory remains stuck in its basic differentiations. Thus, it seems to be appropriate not trying to overcome the classical modernization theory by questioning its core assumptions, decon-structing them as mere ascriptions and refusing them as eurocentrically, teleologically, deter-ministically and nationally narrowed views, but rather developing it further by taking up the articulated criticism. Precisely this is the intention of the present article. At first the core of the modernization approach will be reconstructed in an overview. Hereupon the most important objections to it will be explained. Finally the article presents the design of an original theory of modernity in due consideration of its criticism.
