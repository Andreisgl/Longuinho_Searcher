Sargon used the architecture to show the central, protective, and organizing role of the Assyrian King in the Assyrian state. Its effect was enhanced by planned routes, noises, light and shade effects and gardens. The king was present in his palace either in person or in a stele that was most likely set up in a niche in the throne room. Royal parks were an achievement reserved for the Assyrian king which allowed him to destroy the parks of his enemies.
