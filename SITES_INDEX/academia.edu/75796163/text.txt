Whilst the idea of sinking into a situation is something like a metaphor (in fact more like an instance of Wittgensteinian secondary sense), Jaspers does attempt some characterisation of empathy. It is needed and appropriate for grasping subjective as opposed to objective symptoms. Objective symptoms are those that can be detected by anyone capable either of sense perception or of logical thought. In other words, the detection or comprehension of objective symptoms does not require the possession of any specific kind of mind. In Nagel's phrase, they fit into a view from nowhere .
