L'ombra lunga di Papia tra Filelfo e ForestiRocco BorgognoniThe paper examines the reception of a medieval myth of the foundation of Ancona by the Greek people of the Dolopes. This stemmed from an entry in Papias’ Elementarium, and it was later independently taken up by both Francesco Filelfo in a poem in praise of Felice Ferretti (1433) and Giacomo Filippo Foresti in his Supplementum chronicarum (1483). In search of the channels through which the Dolopian tradition was passed on, it is suggested that Filelfo consulted the ms. Laur. Plut. 27 sin. 3 of the S. Croce library in Florence, while Foresti certainly owned Papias’ editio princeps. In addition, an explanatory conjecture on the attribution of the information in the Supplementum to an unknown «Carinus historicus» instead of Papias is also put forward.
