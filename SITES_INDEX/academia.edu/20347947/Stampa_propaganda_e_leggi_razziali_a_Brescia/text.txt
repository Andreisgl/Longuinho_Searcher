This paper aims to outline the tools and communication context through which an Italian small-town's newspaper worked to create a racist climate in 1938. The first part of the paper (§ 1-2) identifies two sets of binary oppositions – distance/proximity and information/propaganda – which characterize the newspaper’s structure. The second part (§ 3) outlines the informational context in which the racist campaign arises. The third part (§ 4-8) analyzes the chronology and communication techniques of the anti-Semitic campaign conducted by the Piacenza newspaper in the summer of 1938. Immagine, classicità e questione della razza nella propaganda dell’Italia fascista, Cleup, Padova 2011 (ISBN 978-88-6129-651-0)Ciro  ParodoThe myth of ancient Rome is an efficacious rhetorical means through which the Fascism justifies its authority and political programme. Thanks to this generality, the idea of Romanità is transformed into the most exploited theme of the Regime’s propaganda, because it is the most efficacious in arousing broad and popular consensus. So, the Roman antiquity is used often by Fascist rhetoric for its ideological purposes. They are the call for national unity and the legitimacy of imperial aspirations, especially after the conquest of Ethiopia and the proclamation of the Italian Empire (1936), and the justification of racist theories, thanks to the periodicals sponsored by the Regime, particularly the notorious “La Difesa della Razza” (1938-1943).Download Free PDFView PDFSpeciale G.
