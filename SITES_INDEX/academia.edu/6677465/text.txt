Atti del Congresso Internazionale di Studi, Roma, Istituto Nazionale di Studi Romani, 19-20 aprile 2012 Il tesoro monetale di Suq el Kedim (Misurata, Libia)2015 • Salvatore GarraffoThe Treasure of Roman nummi (folles) discovered on 17 February 1981 at Suq el Kedim, near Misurata (Libya), is one of the major finds of silvered bronze coins struck in the Late Roman Empire (more than 108.000 items dating from 294 to ca. 333/4). In this paper Salvatore Garraffo, charged by Libyan Authorities with the study of the Treasure, gives the latest research’s outcomes and presents in detail the remains of the ancient building found in the same archaeological site, the mapping of the jars of the Treasure, the features and the chronology of all groups of coins as well as their minting areas. The author clears up the relationship of Misurata Treasure with the almost contemporary hoards of Colonia and Nagytéténi, dealing with the precise dating of these hoards and their historical context.
