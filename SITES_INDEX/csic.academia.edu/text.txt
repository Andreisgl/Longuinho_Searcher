Database of Neo-Sumerian Texts (BDTNS), http://bdtns.filol.csic.esThe Database of Neo-Sumerian Texts has been developed by Manuel Molina at the Centro de Ciencias ... more The Database of Neo-Sumerian Texts has been developed by Manuel Molina at the Centro de Ciencias Humanas y Sociales of the Consejo Superior de Investigaciones Científicas (Madrid). The aim of the project has been the creation of an open database able to manage more than 95,700 administrative cuneiform tablets written in the Sumerian language (c. 85,000 published, and 10,700 unpublished). These tablets belong to the Neo-Sumerian period (c. 2100-2000 BC), coming basically from five southern cities of Ancient Mesopotamia –Ur, Nippur, Drehem, Girsu and Umma–, and to a minor extent from some other urban settlements of the Neo-Sumerian period.Save to LibraryEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact
