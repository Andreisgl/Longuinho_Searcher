Martin Dibelius calls Luke even ‘Der erste... more Two words are used for Luke: historian and theologian. Martin Dibelius calls Luke even ‘Der erste christliche Historiker’ , Daniel Marguerat follows him therein, as become clear from the title of his book, published in 2002: ‘The First Christian Historian’. Howard Marshall manages both notions for Luke. The book, published in 1970, is called ‘Luke: historian and theologian’. The question is, what's the relationship between both designations? Can Luke be both: historian and theologian? With the term ‘historian a perspective is presupposed shared by everyone, while the term 'theologian' presupposes a faithful perspective. This article deals with this tension. It's a tension the reader meets directly at the beginning of Luke's book, when he reads after the general prologue (1,1-4) the gospel (1,5-24,53). 'Barmhartigheid' in Lucas-Handelingen: Een bijbels-theologische studie, in: www.nicoriemersma.nl (blog d.d. 19 november 2016)'Compassion in Luke-Acts: A Biblical-Theological Study '.  In this biblical-theological article w... more 'Compassion in Luke-Acts: A Biblical-Theological Study '.  In this biblical-theological article we study the way in which Luke works out the theme of mercy in Luke-Acts. The evangelist uses three wordgroups: (I) ἔλεος (6x), ἐλεέω (4x) and ἐλεημοσύνη (10x) (II) οἰκτίρμων (2x), (III) σπλάγχνα (1x) and σπλαγχνίζομαι (3x). The verb σπλαγχνίζομαι is the opus internum, it is the expression of the internal feeling, ποιέω ἔλεος, the opus ad extra, expresses the concrete mercy. To give voice to the divine and the human mercy Luke uses different words. The divine mercy, he expresses with ἔλεος, the human mercy with ἐλεημοσύνη. We pay attention to all occurrences of these words, divided over the occurences where there is a matter of divine mercy, by which he is starting his gospel, and of human mercy, asked and unasked; not answered positively by Abraham in his response to the rich man, but so by Jesus in his response to the call for mercy by the blind beggar and by the ten lepers. We end with the ultimate story of mercy in Luke-Acts: the parable of the merciful Samaritan. There is enough reason to classify this story under the divine mercy! A new version of this paper, in: Het Lucasevangelie onder de loep: Opbouw, stijl en theologie, Vught 2018, 182-204.For a translation in English, use: https://www.deepl.com/nl/translator.Save to LibraryDownloadEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact

Elia, Johannes en Jezus in lucaans perspectief, in: N.A. Riemersma (red.), Lucas & Handelingen (ACEBT 29), Bergambacht 2014, 77-88The picture of Elijah in Luke-Acts is complex. Elijah appears once as an independent story charac... more The picture of Elijah in Luke-Acts is complex. Elijah appears once as an independent story character (in Luke 9:30) in a scene together with Jesus. So Jesus cannot be an appearance of Elijah, as some people of the crowd suppose (9:8,19).  All other times, the name of Elijah sounds from the mouth of story characters.  John (the Baptist) is associated with Elijah a few times, explicitly in 1:17 and implicitly in 1:76 and 7:27, by authoritative persons (the angel Gabriel, the spirit-filled Zacharias and Jesus). So the reader has to listen carefully to what is said about John in relationship to Elijah. According to Jesus, John is the forerunner and preparer of the way of the Lord (= Jesus) in the way Malachi speaks (3:1). Here, Jesus identifies John with this messenger, i.e. Elijah, (see Mal 3:23 = 4:5). Also Jesus is associated with Elijah, explicitly in 4:25,26; 9:8,19 and implicitly in 7:11-17; 9:51-55 and 24:50-53 + Acts 1:1-11. But he is not associated with the Elijah figure of Malachi, but with the Elijah of 1 Kings 17 – 2 Kings 2. Sometimes there is an identification of Jesus with Elijah (7:15), another time Jesus does not resemble Elijah at all (9:54-55). The identification of John with Elijah of Malachi is greater than those of Jesus with the Elijah of the Book of Kings.For a translation in English, use: https://www.deepl.com/nl/translator.Save to LibraryDownloadEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact
