This list is meant as a helpful guide for editors in deciding where to spend time in order to improve their wikipedia's score. The formula to select the top ten articles is not scientific and is subject to change. The formula currently considers three factors when selecting the top ten:
Popularity - you will most likely find working on popular articles easier. So, if a wiki has a smaller article than the average,  it is factor. (See below for the articles listed by average size).
Edge Factor - some articles with little more content will be moved to the next score classification. You can see the size after the article in the top ten list to understand how close it is to the next level.
Runt Factor -  the smallest article (excluding absent ones) is given importance since there could be a problem to correct (e.g. is redirect or micro-stub) and truly is neglected.
