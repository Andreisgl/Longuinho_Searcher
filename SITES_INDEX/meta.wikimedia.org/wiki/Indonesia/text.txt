This page documents the existence of the Wikimedia movement in Indonesia: the world's largest island country, the 14th-largest country by land area, and world's fourth-most populous country.
Major entities within the Wikimedia movement in Indonesia are the projects, affiliate, and like-minded partner organizations.

Projects[edit]
Indonesia has more than 700 local languages. Bahasa Indonesia is the national language.
As of 17 June 2021, there are 12 languages of Indonesia that has at least one Wikimedia project; in total, there are 21 projects in the languages of Indonesia. Several other languages are still in the Wikimedia Incubator. Those projects are denoted in italic.

Affiliate[edit]
Wikimedia Indonesia (WMID) is the Wikimedia affiliate in Indonesia. The chapter was established on September 2008 and has its national office in Jakarta. It is part of the East, Southeast Asia and the Pacific (ESEAP) Hub. 
WMID currently supports the presence of local Wikimedia communities in five Indonesian cities: Jakarta, Yogyakarta, Bandung, Padang, and Denpasar.
Partners[edit]
Creative Commons Indonesia (CCID) is the Creative Commons local chapter for Indonesia.
OpenStreetMap Indonesia is the OpenStreetMap local entity in Indonesia.
Events[edit]
WikiNusantara, the national conference of Indonesian Wikimedians:

Jakarta hosted the 2012 Creative Commons Asia Pacific Conference.
Bali hosted the 2018 Wikimedia ESEAP Conference.
