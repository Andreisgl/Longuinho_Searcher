In the paper the attempt to contrast the opportunities in individual and social development in two diverse societies, Sweden and the Republic of Azerbaijan, is done. Intercultural learning is considered as a curriculum theoretical perspective and in a broad sense a didactic perspective. Ordinlärning i L2-svenska  En studie om lexical inferencing och strategianvändning av  andraspråkselever på Språkintroduktion och gymnasietOrdinlärning i L2-svenska  En studie om lexical inferencing och strategianvändning av  andraspråkselever på Språkintroduktion och gymnasiet,  2021In this essay, I examine the learning of Swedish as a second language in two groups of young peop... more In this essay, I examine the learning of Swedish as a second language in two groups of young people at a high school in Sweden. The focus of the study is on the use of word learning strategies in second language learning by these informants, i.e. what types of strategies and how learners' use of these strategies can be described with special focus on determining the meanings of unknown words. The focus is also on the importance that the use of these strategies has on the informants' results. Special focus is placed on the use of strategies when students derive and/ or generate the meanings of words unknown to them based on the context during the reading process, which is called lexical inferencing (LIF) in linguistics. The study applies the think-aloud method for examiningthe informants' skills in lexical inferencing, i.e. the informants orally inform their thoughts and conclusions about the meanings of the unknown words in the given reading passage. The study analyzes and discusses and discusses why certain strategies are used and what implications these may have for more or less successful word learning. The results of the study show i.a. that the informants use of the whole spectrum of cognitive, metacognitive, social and affective strategies, but that they use these strategies to varying degrees and to varying degrees of skill. The results of the study also show that it is difficult to say anything unambiguous about the relationship between the use of learning strategies and success in LIF or about covariation between the strategies, but that there are indications of covariation between cognitive and social strategies, between cognitive and metacognitive strategies and between affective and metacognitive strategies. There are also indications of how these affect the success of LIF and the attempt to explainwhy these covariations lead to more or less successful derivation is made in the analysis and discussion part of the study.Save to LibraryDownloadEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact

Like many poets of his day, Griboyedov died young. In 1829 he was murdered in Tehran. But not many people know that Griboyedov was a Russian diplomat. He was actually Russian ambassador to Iran in 1828- 1829. He was Russia’s ambassador to Qajar Persia. Qajar dynasty was a royal dynasty of Turkic origin, they were namely Azerbaijanis and they rulled over the region which you call Iran today from 1789 to 1925.  Griboyedov was the person who had played a pivotal role in the ratification of the Treaty of Turkmenchay (1828). Griboyedov had played an active role in negotiating the terms of the treaty. The Treaty of Turkmenchay was an agreement between Qajar Iran and the Russian Empire, which concluded the Russo-Persian War (1826–28). Griboyedov played a role in the occupation of Azerbaijan. Griboyedov's story actually begins in 1812. Griboyedov was Paskevich's diplomatic adviser, and the Russian army celebrated the victory in Yerevan by staging the play Woe from Wit. From the late nineteenth century until the present, the story of industrial civilization as we know it is the story of oil and petroleum, so called black gold. Based on this reason, one of the central places in the world has been Azerbaijan, which constitutes the south border between Europe and Asia. The purpose of this investigation is to explore the outbreaks affecting Azerbaijan, to explore a causative effect between the industrial and geopolitical dynamics surrounding the country and the political and ethnic violence that ravaged it. Furthermore, the paper will address how position of Baku as "the oil kingdom" was additionally complicated by the geopolitical interference caused by the First World War and the issue of Nagorno-Karabakh and their aftermath. The city of Baku and surrounding area is one of the best-known oil regions in the world. This work aims to analyze and illustrate how the industrialization of oil secured Baku as a place of immense importance in the global economic order of the period. In order to accomplish this task, this study will examine elements of social, economic, and political history across the late nineteenth, twentieth and early twenty first centuries in its analysis of the factors influencing the Republic of Azerbaijan as well as the neighboring states. These elements continually interacted with the process of industrial production and political economy that defined the place and period.Save to LibraryDownloadEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact
