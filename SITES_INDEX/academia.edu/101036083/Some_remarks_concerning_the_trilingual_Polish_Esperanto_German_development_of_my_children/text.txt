Abstract. In order to typologically describe Esperanto and find a place for it among natural languages, it is necessary to examine it according to diverse criteria that are applied to ethnic languages and to compare it with the gamut of natural languages. Various criteria can be found for different levels of language description and these are explored in the WALS database, although they do not apply to all levels of all languages. Statistical analyses of Esperanto according to these criteria are important and interesting although they do not definitively locate Esperanto. The questions of internationality and ease of acquisition of Esperanto remain unsolved. The goal of the present study is to find an answer based on selected elements of phonetics (the sound inventory and the ways its elements are combined), syntax (expression of syntactic relations, agreement, word order), morphology (the repertoire of morphemes and their productive combinations), semantics (semantic compositionality of meanings, segmentation of the world by words, for example segmentation of the color spectrum), and pragmatics (the expression of politeness through pronouns).
