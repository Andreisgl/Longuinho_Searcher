This manuscript (dated 1203) is the only surviving version of the work Mubtada' al-dunya wa-qiṣaṣ al-anbiyā' (The Beginning of the World and Prophets' Stories) by Isḥāq Abū Ḥudhayfa b. Bishr al-Qurashī (d. 821). Only the first volume of the work has survived in the codex (bound manuscript). This volume covers the stories of (in order, not exhaustive) the creation and Adam, Cain and Abel, Idris, Noah, Hud, Salih, Abraham and Lot. Isḥāq b. Bishr's work has been described by researchers as the first of its kind (prophet stories as a genre) to survive (esp., Tottoli, 2001). This is one of the main reasons why the importance of its edition has been underlined (M. Pregill, et al., 2017). Therefore, this dissertation is devoted to making this work more accessible, by means of a partial edition and translation, in the hope of providing a useful version that might serve as a starting point for the publication of the entire text.More concretely, this Master's thesis contains the edition and translation of only one of the chapters from the work; that of Noah (found on folios 99 to 114 in the manuscript). In summary, this chapter deals with Noah's call to prophethood, the confrontation with his people, the building of the ark, the story of the flood, the repopulation of the earth, genealogies of all nations from the sons of Noah, and other details, such as a conversation between the devil and Noah.The edition and translation are preceded by an introduction, which discusses the life and work of Isḥāq b. bishr; his Mubtada' al-dunya wa-qiṣaṣ al-anbiyā' and some notes on its structure, sources and distribution; information about the manuscript and observations about its orthographic particularities; and a justification for the way in which I arrived at the edition and translation.Keywords: Qiṣaṣ al-anbiyā', prophetic stories, Isḥāq b. Several rewritings of this text – some substantial – and the prompt composition of new ones testify to the extent to which Adalbert’s life and death was able to bring focus to the disputes of the time. What to make of his desertion – albeit pious – of his episcopacy? What was the reason for Adalbert’s death? How does one proceed with a missionary endeavor? Who are the pagans?
