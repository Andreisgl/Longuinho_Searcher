With a share of about 29.9% of GDP, the, crude oil and associated support activities are a major source of funding of the domestic economy. The aim of this study is to identify the effects of the price of oil on net operating revenue of the principals Angolan banking institutions from 2010 to 2015. The institutions assigned to the study were selected according to the ranking of stratification of total assets, released by Deloitte Auditing Company in its annual publication BancaemAnálise, ed. 2015, namely the Banks BPC, BAI, BFA, BIC and the BPA. The theoretical framework focused on conceptual review of the components of net operating revenue and analysis, principally, of the main causes of crude oil price fall in the international market. The research was based on primary and secondary data obtained through annul reports of the mentioned institutions as well as information published by BNA, KPMG, Deloitte, ABANC, EIA and so many financial quote and education websites.For the statistical analysis, we apply linear regression and af-terthe multiple regression. In the first one, the results showed that there is a moderatenegative linear relationship between the net operating revenue and the Brent crude oil price and,also, that Brent crude oil priceonly explains about 48.30% of the variability of net operating income – which suggests a set of variables involved on the behavior of the explained/dependent variable, among which the exchange rate. After the association of this variable (as an inde-pendent variable) to the multiple regression model, the results show that there is a very strong positive linear relationship among the net operating income, Brent crude oil price and the exchange rate and 94.10% of the variability of net operating revenue is explained by the two explanatory variables, i.e., the Brent crude oil price and the exchange rate, and the 5.90% is explained by other factors not considered in the cited model.
