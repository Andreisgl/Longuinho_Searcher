Folklore StudiesThe article is devoted to the analysis of Arnold van Gennep&#39;s study of the invariant structure of funeral rituals in his famous work &quot;The Rites of Passage&quot; (1909). In the scientific concept of the scientist, the rites of passage (initiation rites) accompany the most important periods and events of human life and are associated with changes in the position (including social) and status of an individual. Analyzing the funeral cycle, A. van Gennep emphasizes that preliminar rites (&quot;rites of separation from the previous world&quot;) are the least pronounced, liminar rites (&quot;rites performed in the intermediate period&quot;) are long and complex, and the most important and most developed are postliminar rites (&quot;rites of inclusion in the new world&quot;) which are carried out to incorporate the dead to the world of the dead.
