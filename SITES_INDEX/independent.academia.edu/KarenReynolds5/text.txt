A major figure of the second half of the twenti... more Toru Takemitsu died in Tokyo on 20 February 1996. A major figure of the second half of the twentieth century, he was essentially self-taught. His work extended from a large collection of essays and imaginative fiction through a career-long engagement with film scoring to his central achievement as a composer, a body of music that, while emphasizing the orchestral dimension in which he was a prolific master, included also fragile and exquisite pieces on a smaller scale.
