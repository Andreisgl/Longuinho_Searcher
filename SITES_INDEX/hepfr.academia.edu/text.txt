Zum Verhältnis von Fachdidaktik und Fachwissenschaftby Petra  Bleisch Bouzar and Ansgar JödickeZeitschrift für Religionskunde,  2022In the development of a didactic of religious studies at school the question arises, among other ... more In the development of a didactic of religious studies at school the question arises, among other things, what relationship this didactic should have to the associated academic disciplines. The article examines this relationship by means of an example, namely the didactical methods of interviewing and observation in school lessons. The article compares the use of these methods in four different contexts: the Swiss national curriculum „Lehrplan 21“, the Swiss textbook „Blickpunkt“, the methodology of social sciences/academic study of religions, and the didactic concept of inquiry-based learning. The comparison results in two findings: both, the curriculum and the textbook would profit from the model of inquiry-based learning which is well informed regarding the social sciences’ methodology. Furthermore, the comparison enables a more nuanced relationship between social sciences/academic studies of religions and didactics of religious studies at school.Save to LibraryDownloadEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact

The 'world religions paradigm' as the organising principle of didactic action-a case studyBritisch Journal for Religious Education,  2021This article shows how discussion of the 'world religions paradigm' in integrated natural and soc... more This article shows how discussion of the 'world religions paradigm' in integrated natural and social science teaching affects how religious plurality is handled. A concrete case study is used to illustrate how a teacher designs lessons on world religions. Based on this single case study, the article argues that the use of specific teaching materials, which are shaped according to the world religions paradigm and contain a highly essentialised and ahistorical depiction of religions, contrasts with the more nuanced additions made by the teacher in the oral monologue parts of the lesson. It is shown that the world religions paradigm is subtly and implicitly questioned as well as reproduced by the teacher. The analysis is based on de Certeau's theory of strategies and tactics, which allows for a discussion of teachers' scope of action.Save to LibraryDownloadEditCompare Citation Rank Readers Related Papers MentionsView Impact

(ECR) » dans des écoles primaires en Suisse romandeZeitschrift für Religionskunde,  2021In Switzerland, the religious landscape has considerably
changed in the past decades towards a si... more In Switzerland, the religious landscape has considerablychanged in the past decades towards a significantdiversity of religious and non-religious people. Onthe educational level, this situation has resulted by theintroduction of new school subjects in the curriculum,among which the subject “ethics and religious cultures”– éthique et cultures religieuses – (ERC) in the Frenchspeaking part of the country. Working from the actors’discourses gathered via interviews, this article focuseson the conceptions of religious diversity held by primaryschool teachers in the cantons of Fribourg, Vaudand Valais in their professional context and analyzeshow these conceptions impact the way the teachersapproach the ERC subject. We observe, among otherthings, that for most teachers the relevance of ERCteaching (in terms of usefulness and interest) and thepresence of a certain diversity (religious and of origins)within the students are tightly linked. Our analyses alsoshow that the conception of diversity held by the teachersinfluences the way they perceive and deal with thediversity present in the classroom. For some, diversityrepresents challenges – more or less inconvenient on asocial and professional level, for others it represents arich learning opportunity. The heroine and the adventurer: Narratives of acquisition and production of Islamic normative orders. The case of Swiss converts to Islam.This contribution investigates the acquisition and production of Islamic normative orders in West... more This contribution investigates the acquisition and production of Islamic normative orders in Western Europe. Thereby, “Islamic normative orders” are understood as religious “Sollensordnungen” (Kunz & Mona 2006), which not only regulate what Muslims should do but also how the world should be and how the world should be perceived (Schlieter 2012). As in Western Europe (as elsewhere), Islamic normativities are not given and Muslims also orient themselves by non-Islamic normative orders, the questions can be raised, what female Muslim converts understand by Islamic normativities, how they acquire and produce Islamic normativities and how non-Islamic normative orders interfere in this processes. Using the concept of micronarratives as a new approach in the field of anthropology, cognitive psychology and narratology, it is argued, that the acquisition of Islamic normative orders is narrated in two clearly distinguishable narrative schemes, romance and comedy (White 1973; 1986). They differ in both, the conceptualization of Islamic normativities as well as the narrated interference of non-Islamic normative orders.
