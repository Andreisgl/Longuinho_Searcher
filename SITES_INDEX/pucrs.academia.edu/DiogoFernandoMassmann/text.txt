BooksThe Problems of Consciousness and the Cognitive ModelsContemporary Philosophy,  2022ABSTRACT: Background The task of examining consciousness is a diverse project and in the literatu... more ABSTRACT: Background The task of examining consciousness is a diverse project and in the literature, it is divided into descriptive, explanatory and functional questions. By definition, the three questions consist of describing the characteristics of consciousness, explaining its underlying basis or cause, and answering the question about the role or value of consciousness. This paper is going to focus on the functional issue regarding the question of the existence of consciousness. Cognitivist models of consciousness share some principles, however, there is no consensus on how this occurs. There are rival models that differently address how modularity and interconnectedness are related to cognition and consciousness. Two of them are the Neural Global Workspace Model (GNW) and the Recurrent Processing (RPM). Of special importance is the claim that consciousness, in both the accessing and phenomenal senses, occurs when and only when relevant content enters the GNW, involving the primary sensory areas as well as many other areas including frontal and frontal. parietal associated with attention. However, RPM argues that first-order representations and meta-representations may be sufficient for phenomenal consciousness. These results have consequences for the philosophical debate observed between advocates of first-order and higher-order theories. Methodology: This is a basic research approach that seeks to establish, from a set of explanatory models, conceptual definitions that can be used in applied science. Objectives: Initially, present the problem of the function of consciousness and the cognitive models, then, in the second section, present the cognitive models of GNW and RPM. In a third moment, show some predictions about these models for the philosophical debate between first and high-order theories. Results: Despite their incompleteness and explanatory limitations, as these models lack a greater theoretical foundation and experimental corroboration, such models are prospects for research on consciousness.
