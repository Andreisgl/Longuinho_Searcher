In the fluvial environment water availability is a crucial issue. The paper provides both broader and stricter definitions for a new concept in the environmental assessment of rivers and their floodplains, environmental flow, and attempts to distinguish it from similar concepts. A classification and brief presentation of the most widespread and relevant methods for rating and analyzing environmental flow in international literature follows. Special attention is paid to the contributions geomorphologists make to the ecological investigations of river channels and riparian zones.
