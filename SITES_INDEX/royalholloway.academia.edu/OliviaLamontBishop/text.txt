Olivia Lamont BishopRoyal Holloway, University of London,  Department of Drama, Theatre and Dance,  Graduate StudentHistory and Theory of Scenography+4I am a fully funded PhD student in theatre and performance. My research addresses the representation of place in relation to conflict and migration in performances in the UK. I am the Project Assistant for the Migrant Dramaturgies Network and a member of the Theatre and Performance Research Association (TaPRA).I graduated from the University of Warwick in 2012 with a First Class degree in Theatre and Performance Studies, receiving the Clive Barker Award for Performance. I later received a Masters from Utrecht University in Theatre Studies, where my thesis concerned the exploration of place and mapping in the work of choreographer Dan Canham.I have created socially engaged performances, talks and installations for Battersea Arts Centre, DICE Festival, Edinburgh Festival Fringe, the International Community Arts Festival and Take Me Somewhere Festival (Beyond Strangers).
