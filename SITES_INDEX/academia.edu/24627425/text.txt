Reading the Way to the Netherworld Education and the Representations of the Beyond in Later Antiquity, V&R, 2016Anna LefteratouNonnus' Dionysiaca is a lengthy and complex epic account of Dionysus' ancestry and exploits, following the main stylistic principle of poikilia. This principle of variation, illustrated in Nonnus' programmatic evocation of Proteus in the proem, is employed not only for stylistic purposes, but applies to the content as well, leading thus to a variety of interpretational paths.1 Nonnus' Dionysiaca and its configuration of the Beyond have been a subject for intense debate between scholars who assume the poem to have Orphic, Christian, or other aspirations.2 In this volume, however, Marta Otlewska-Jung successfully demonstrates the generic nature of Nonnus' Underworld and how it is continuously adapted each time a character dies or is metamorphosed, making thus death a personal issue.
