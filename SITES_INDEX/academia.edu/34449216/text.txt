М. 1998Наталья ЕниосоваArchaeological excavations at the Viking Age settlement at Gnezdovo provide a large body of new information about the jewellery manufacture at this site. Casting moulds discussed in the present paper fall into two groups. The first group of stone items includes four objects. They have been made of soft fine-grained sedimentary rocks well suited for carving. The absence of any traces of using have shown that stone moulds could have been intended for casting of metal with low melting point. The second group is of clay items used for casting brooches attributed to Scandinavia. Clay material was analysed by chemical and microscopically methods. All clay fragments from Gnezdovo belong to the piece-moulds made of two fitted valves. They testify the piece-mould casting technique had been brought to Gnezdovo by Scandinavian artisans.
