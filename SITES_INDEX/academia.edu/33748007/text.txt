Benedetto di Salerno2019 • Paolo PedutoThe identification of the remains of a late-antique baptistery placed ad corpus outside the walls of the Roman Salernum, allows a better understanding of the evolution of the early Christian city that developed within the walls through the occupation of public spaces in which private citizens made oratories and cemetery churches, while outside similar structures overlapped to the previous Roman cemeteries where relics of saints are often found. The identification of the central plan architecture, together with 5th-6th century epigraphic findings underlying the subsequent church of S. Benedetto, provided the opportunity to reflect on the evolution of similar architectures in the city of Salerno from the Middle Ages to present day.Download Free PDFView PDFStudi in Memoria di Roma (Peduto Natella)Paolo PedutoDownload Free PDFView PDFTra Nola e Cimitile: alla ricerca della prima cattedrale, in «Rassegna Storica Salernitana», n.s., XXIV/1 (47) (giugno 2007), pp. 25-119Carlo EbanistaDownload Free PDFView PDFSee Full PDFDownload PDFLoading PreviewSorry, preview is currently unavailable.
